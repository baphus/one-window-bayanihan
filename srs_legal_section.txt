The system shall comply with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173), it's Implementing Rules and Regulations, and applicable National Privacy Commission guidance.Compliance obligations shall include:
LEGAL-001Personal data shall be processed only for legitimate, authorized, and operationally necessary purposes related to OFW assistance coordination, case management, referral processing, monitoring, and service delivery.
LEGAL-002Collection, storage, access, transmission, and processing of OFW personal data shall be limited to the minimum necessary information required for legitimate operational purposes.
LEGAL-003Personally identifiable information and sensitive case data shall be protected through technical, organizational, and administrative safeguards.
LEGAL-004Access to personal information shall be restricted to authorized users with legitimate operational need.
LEGAL-005The system shall support auditability, accountability, and traceability for protected data access and operational actions.
LEGAL-006Data retention, archival, disposal, and record lifecycle handling shall follow documented governance controls.
LEGAL-007Unauthorized disclosure, misuse, or uncontrolled exposure of OFW personal information shall be prohibited.
6.1.2 Department of Migrant Workers Act Compliance (Republic Act No. 11641)
The system shall align with the governance mandate established under Republic Act No. 11641.
Compliance obligations shall include:
LEGAL-008The Department of Migrant Workers (DMW) shall remain the primary operational authority for centralized OFW case governance.
LEGAL-009Official case intake and centralized case lifecycle governance shall remain under authorized DMW personnel.
LEGAL-010Participating partner agencies shall operate within delegated referral and intervention responsibilities without assuming unrestricted centralized case governance authority.
LEGAL-011Inter-agency workflows shall preserve accountability, visibility control, and governance oversight consistent with DMW authority.
6.1.3 DICT Cloud First Policy Compliance
Bayanihan One Window shall align with the principles of the DICT Cloud First Policy through managed cloud service adoption, subject to governance and risk controls appropriate for protected operational data.
Compliance obligations shall include:
LEGAL-012Cloud-hosted infrastructure may be used where security, privacy, governance, availability, and operational suitability requirements are satisfied.
LEGAL-013Managed commercial cloud services used by the system shall be formally approved as part of deployment governance.
LEGAL-014Cloud adoption shall be accompanied by documented risk assessment and privacy governance review.
LEGAL-015Security responsibilities across application, storage, database, and vendor-managed infrastructure shall be clearly defined.
LEGAL-016Cloud service dependencies shall be documented as operational infrastructure assumptions.
6.1.4 Cross-Border Processing and International Data Governance
Because Bayanihan One Window uses managed commercial cloud services including Render, Supabase, Supabase Storage, and other external service providers, certain technical operations may involve infrastructure outside Philippine jurisdiction.
Potential exposure scenarios include:
managed hosting infrastructure 
database replication or backups 
service telemetry 
vendor administrative access 
content delivery infrastructure 
messaging provider communications 
AI API traffic (if enabled) 
Accordingly, the system shall operate under regulated cross-border processing governance.
LEGAL-017Cross-border processing involving regulated OFW personal information shall be subject to documented privacy governance controls.
LEGAL-018Use of third-party infrastructure providers shall require documented organizational approval.
LEGAL-019Third-party providers handling protected operational data shall be subject to confidentiality and privacy obligations.
LEGAL-020Vendor access to protected production information shall be controlled, restricted, and operationally justified.
LEGAL-021Sensitive OFW personal data shall not be unnecessarily exposed to third-party services.
LEGAL-022International processing risks shall be evaluated through privacy and operational governance review.
6.1.5 Privacy Impact Assessment Requirements
Because the system handles protected personal and case information through commercial cloud infrastructure, privacy risk governance shall be mandatory.
LEGAL-023A Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) or equivalent privacy risk evaluation shall be maintained for the deployment architecture.
The privacy assessment shall evaluate:
personal data exposure risks 
unauthorized access risks 
cloud infrastructure risks 
foreign jurisdiction risks 
vendor support access risks 
document exposure risks 
messaging provider risks 
AI service risks (if enabled) 
breach response considerations 
LEGAL-024Material architectural changes affecting protected data processing shall require updated privacy review.
6.1.6 Third-Party Service Governance
The system depends on managed external providers.
Examples include:
Render 
Supabase
Supabase Storage
email/OTP providers 
AI providers (if enabled) 
The following governance obligations shall apply.
LEGAL-025Third-party service providers shall only be used for legitimate operational purposes.
LEGAL-026Service providers shall not be treated as unrestricted controllers of OFW operational data.
LEGAL-027Access permissions granted to third-party services shall be limited to required operational functionality.
LEGAL-028Provider credentials, service secrets, and privileged access mechanisms shall be securely governed.
LEGAL-029Provider dependency risk shall be acknowledged within operational governance planning.
6.1.7 Procurement and Public Sector Governance Alignment
Where Bayanihan One Window is evaluated for institutional deployment, infrastructure and technical services shall be assessed in accordance with applicable public-sector governance and procurement expectations.
LEGAL-030Infrastructure architecture shall be documented clearly enough for technical review, audit, and procurement evaluation.
LEGAL-031Security controls, vendor dependencies, operational assumptions, and cloud architecture constraints shall be explicitly documented.
LEGAL-032Technology decisions shall preserve maintainability, auditability, and operational accountability.
6.1.8 AI Governance Requirements (If Enabled)
Where AI-assisted chatbot functionality is enabled:
LEGAL-033AI integrations shall be restricted to legitimate approved operational functions.
LEGAL-034Protected OFW personal information shall not be unnecessarily transmitted to AI services.
LEGAL-035Sensitive case narratives, personal identifiers, documentary evidence, and privileged inter-agency operational records shall not be exposed to AI services without explicit governance approval.
LEGAL-036AI usage shall remain subordinate to privacy, operational, and security governance requirements.
6.2 Database Integrity and Processing Requirements
The database shall preserve transactional correctness for all operational workflows involving case intake, referral processing, milestone recording, case closure, document association, and audit persistence.
DB-001The PostgreSQL environment shall enforce ACID-compliant transaction processing.
