1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for Bayanihan One Window Version 1.0, a secure, web-based inter-agency referral and tracking system developed to support the coordinated assistance of distressed Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) in Region VII.
The system is intended to streamline case intake, referral coordination, monitoring, and closure by establishing a centralized single-entry mechanism under the Department of Migrant Workers (DMW) Region VII. Through the creation of a Unified Master Case File, the platform enables authorized participating agencies to coordinate assistance through a shared digital case management environment while maintaining centralized oversight by DMW.
The scope of this SRS covers the complete Version 1.0 software product, including OFW intake and case profiling, case documentation, inter-agency referral management, intervention tracking, document management, OFW case status monitoring, AI-assisted inquiry support, analytics and reporting, administrative management, and case closure processes.
Bayanihan One Window operates as a standalone coordination platform and does not include direct integration with the internal operational systems or databases of participating partner agencies. Updates and service interventions from partner agencies are recorded manually by authorized agency personnel within the system.
This document serves as the baseline reference for the system’s analysis, design, implementation, testing, deployment, and future maintenance.
1.2 Document Conventions
The following conventions and standards are used throughout this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) to ensure consistency, clarity, and traceability:
“Shall” indicates a mandatory requirement that must be implemented for the system to satisfy this specification. 
“Should” indicates a recommended requirement that is desirable but may be deferred based on implementation constraints or project priorities. 
“May” indicates an optional capability, enhancement, or implementation consideration. 
Requirement statements are assigned unique identifiers to support traceability across analysis, design, implementation, and testing. Structured prefixes are used according to requirement classification, including:
FR – Functional Requirements 
NFR – Non-Functional Requirements 
SEC – Security Requirements 
UI – User Interface Requirements 
REP – Reporting Requirements 
AUD – Audit and Logging Requirements 
Each requirement statement is assigned its own identifier and shall be treated independently for prioritization, validation, and traceability.
User roles that represent access-controlled system actors (e.g., DMW CASE MANAGER, PARTNER AGENCY USER, SYSTEM ADMINISTRATOR) are written in uppercase for consistency and clear distinction from general descriptive text.
Official agency names, system modules, and product components are written in Title Case throughout this document for naming consistency.
The term Lane-Based Routing refers to the controlled inter-agency referral model in which case information is accessible only to authorized agencies within their assigned workflow boundaries, in accordance with access control and privacy rules.
Non-functional requirements in this SRS are categorized in alignment with the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 software quality model, including functional suitability, performance efficiency, compatibility, interaction capability, reliability, security, maintainability, flexibility, and safety.
Example requirement format:
FR-REF-101: The system shall allow a DMW CASE MANAGER to create a case record and route it to a partner agency for acceptance.

1.4 Product Scope
Bayanihan One Window is a secure, web-based platform designed to improve the assistance process for distressed Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) in Region VII. It addresses the inefficiencies of manual and fragmented case coordination, where OFWs often need to repeatedly submit documents and explain their situations to multiple agencies.
The system provides a centralized single-entry process managed by the Department of Migrant Workers (DMW) Region VII, where a Unified Master Case File is created and shared with authorized partner agencies involved in providing assistance. This enables faster case handling, better referral coordination, and clearer monitoring of intervention progress.
Bayanihan One Window aims to improve transparency, accountability, response time, and continuity of care by digitizing and standardizing the OFW assistance workflow. Version 1.0 focuses on core case intake, referral management, monitoring, and tracking functions for DMW Region VII and participating partner agencies. The system operates as a standalone coordination platform and does not replace the internal systems of partner institutions.

2. Overall Description
2.1 Product Overview
Bayanihan One Window is a new, standalone web-based information system designed to improve assistance coordination for distressed Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) in Region VII. Rather than replacing the internal systems of participating agencies, it serves as a centralized inter-agency coordination platform that addresses fragmented and manual processes in OFW case handling, referral management, and intervention monitoring.
The system establishes a unified digital environment where the Department of Migrant Workers (DMW) Region VII serves as the primary intake and case governance authority, creating and managing a Unified Master Case File shared with authorized partner agencies. Core functions include case intake, service need documentation, digital referrals, inter-agency case monitoring, milestone tracking, case resolution, analytics reporting, AI chatbot assistance, and a public OFW tracking portal with OTP-secured access.
As part of the broader OFW assistance ecosystem, Bayanihan One Window connects DMW Region VII, partner agencies, OFW clients, OTP authentication services, and AI support services to enable coordinated and transparent service delivery while preserving each agency’s internal operational autonomy.
2.2 Product Functions
Bayanihan One Window provides integrated functions for coordinated case management, inter-agency referral processing, and assistance monitoring for distressed Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) in Region VII. Major product functions include:
Register and manage distressed OFW cases through a centralized intake process. 
Create and maintain a Unified Master Case File containing client information, documents, and case records. 
Record case assessments, identified service needs, and intervention requirements. 
Generate and dispatch digital referrals to appropriate partner agencies. 
Monitor referral progress, agency interventions, and milestone updates. 
Enforce role-based access control and secure case governance across participating agencies. 
Provide AI chatbot assistance for common inquiries and case-related guidance. 
Allow OFWs to securely track case status through a public portal using tracker number and OTP verification. 
Support case resolution, closure validation, and outcome documentation. 
Collect client feedback following service completion. 
Generate analytics dashboards, reports, and performance monitoring data for management oversight.

2.5 Design and Implementation Constraints
Regulatory Compliance Constraint:The system shall comply with Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012), Republic Act No. 11641, applicable National Privacy Commission rules, DICT Cloud First Policy, and applicable public-sector ICT governance standards.
Commercial Cloud Deployment Constraint:The system shall operate using managed commercial cloud infrastructure including Render, Supabase, and Cloudinary.
Cross-Border Processing Constraint:Because commercial cloud providers may process infrastructure metadata, backups, support telemetry, or replicated content outside Philippine jurisdiction, deployment shall be governed under documented cross-border processing safeguards consistent with privacy law.
Vendor Governance Constraint:Third-party providers shall be subject to contractual privacy, confidentiality, access restriction, and data processing obligations.
Privacy Impact Assessment Constraint:Deployment shall require documented privacy risk assessment covering cloud exposure, vendor access risk, international transfer risk, and incident response.
Technology Stack Constraint:Implementation shall use Laravel, React, Inertia.js, Tailwind CSS, PostgreSQL, and approved cloud services.
Standalone Platform Constraint:Version 1.0 shall operate independently without direct integration into legacy partner systems.
Manual Agency Update Constraint:Partner agencies shall manually encode milestone updates and referral actions.
Centralized Intake Constraint:Only authorized DMW personnel shall create official OFW case records.
Lane-Based Access Constraint:Agency users shall only access records assigned to their authorized operational lane.
Parallel Referral Constraint:The system shall support simultaneous referrals to multiple agencies.
Centralized Closure Constraint:Only authorized DMW personnel shall close official cases.
AI Processing Constraint:If AI services are enabled, personal OFW data shall not be transmitted without privacy-approved safeguards.

2.7 Assumptions and Dependencies
2.7.1 Assumed Factors
The requirements stated in this SRS are based on the following assumptions. If these assumptions change or are incorrect, the project scope and functionality may be significantly affected:
Institutional Commitment and Governance: It is assumed that DMW Region VII and all participating partner agencies (OWWA, DOLE, TESDA, DSWD, DOH, etc.) will formally designate Agency Focal Persons and maintain updated service categories and referral pathways.
Manual Data Entry Compliance: Since the system operates as a standalone platform without direct integration into legacy databases, it is assumed that partner agencies will manually record milestone updates and service outcomes within their assigned system "lanes".
User Infrastructure: It is assumed that all authorized personnel and OFWs have access to internet-enabled devices (desktops, laptops, or smartphones) and supported modern web browsers to interact with the platform.
Operational Connectivity: The system assumes the availability of a stable internet connection (at least 15-30 Mbps) at regional and field offices to ensure real-time dashboard updates and secure document handling.
Regulatory Stability: The requirements assume the continued legal mandate of Republic Act No. 11641 and the Data Privacy Act of 2012, which provide the institutional and security framework for the "Single-Entry" intake model.
Staff Capacity: It is assumed that personnel will undergo basic orientation to transition from manual, paper-based, or email-centric workflows to the centralized digital coordination platform.
2.7.2 Dependencies
The project is dependent on the following external factors and third-party components:
Cloud Infrastructure Providers: The system’s availability is directly dependent on Render (for application hosting), Supabase (for the PostgreSQL database), and Cloudinary (for secure media storage and CDN).
AI Service Integration: The AI Chatbot module (UC-23, UC-24) depends on external AI models and APIs (e.g., OpenAI) for processing natural language inquiries and providing service guidance.
Communication Services: Future integrations for automated SMS or advanced messaging depend on the approval of external service providers and the availability of a dedicated operational budget.
Institutional Coordination Protocols: The functional success of the system depends on the establishment of agreed-upon Inter-Agency Protocols that define turnaround times for referral acceptance and service completion.
Security Standards: The technical evaluation of the project is dependent on the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 quality model, which serves as the benchmark for measuring the system's performance, security, and maintainability.
3. External Interface Requirements
3.1 User Interfaces
The Bayanihan One Window system provides role-based web interfaces designed for secure case management and coordinated inter-agency tracking. The logical characteristics of these interfaces are aligned with the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 Interaction Capability standard to ensure they are intuitive, responsive, and efficient.
1. GUI Standards and Style Guide
Design Framework: The system utilizes Tailwind CSS for a consistent visual language and React for a dynamic, responsive user experience.
Layout Constraints: The interface follows a desktop-first design optimized for office-based DMW Case Managers and Agency Focal Persons, while ensuring mobile responsiveness for OFWs accessing the tracking portal via smartphones.
Visual Philosophy: The UI emphasizes a "One OFW, One Entry" model, using a lane-column case tracker to visually separate administrative boundaries (lanes) for different agencies like OWWA, DOLE, and DSWD.
Accessibility Standard: User interface design shall align with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility principles.
2. Standard Buttons and Functions
Every administrative screen shall include the following persistent elements:
Dashboard-First Navigation: A sidebar or top-navigation menu providing instant access to case queues, pending referrals, and audit reports.
AI Chatbot Help: A persistent AI Assistant module (UC-23, UC-24) available on the landing and tracking pages to provide real-time guidance on services and case status inquiries.
Security Checkpoints: Contextual privacy alerts and visual security indicators serve as mandatory "checkpoints" before high-stakes actions, such as initiating an inter-agency referral or closing a case.
3. Logical Characteristics of User Interfaces
The system is partitioned into specific software components requiring distinct user interfaces:
Public Landing Page & OFW Tracking:
Input: Requires a unique Tracker Number.
Security Logic: Implements an OTP Verification screen where the OFW must enter a code sent to their registered email before viewing status.
Privacy-Safe View: Displays a restricted timeline of milestones without exposing confidential internal agency comments or sensitive attachments.
DMW Case Intake & Management:
Intake Forms: Standardized, grouped forms for capturing personal details, vulnerability indicators (e.g., PWD, Solo Parent), and the case narrative.
Referral Dispatch: An interface allowing Case Managers to select multiple partner agencies in parallel and attach specific service-based documents.
Closure Logic: A specialized interface that validates if all agency referrals are in terminal states (Completed/Rejected) before allowing final case closure.
Partner Agency/LGU Lane View:
Lane-Based Interaction: Agency users are restricted to a view of referrals assigned only to their specific "lane".
Milestone Logging: An append-only interface for recording service updates, ensuring that historical progress cannot be edited or deleted.
Administrative & Analytics Dashboard:
User/Agency Management: Interfaces for creating accounts, assigning roles (RBAC), and configuring agency service registries.
Audit Logging: A searchable, filterable table for monitoring all system activities and user actions for full traceability.
4. Error and Validation Standards
Privacy-Safe Messages: Error messages and validation alerts are designed to be non-technical and do not leak sensitive system information to unauthorized users.
Validation Logic: System-managed fields (like Case and Tracker Numbers) are automatically generated to prevent manual entry errors.
Input Safeguards: The UI enforces strict input validation to prevent common vulnerabilities such as SQL injection and Cross-Site Scripting (XSS).

The following performance requirements define the expected operational efficiency of the Bayanihan One Window system in alignment with the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 Performance Efficiency quality characteristic. These requirements support timely inter-agency coordination, reduced administrative delays, and improved responsiveness for distressed OFW assistance workflows.
NFR-PERF-001: Core Interface Response TimeThe system shall load the login interface, dashboard views, and primary navigation pages within 3 seconds under normal operating conditions with a stable internet connection of at least 15 Mbps. 
NFR-PERF-002: Data Retrieval PerformanceThe system shall retrieve and display filtered case records, referral queues, and case monitoring views within 5 seconds under normal operating conditions. 
NFR-PERF-003: Real-Time Synchronization PerformanceReferral status updates, milestone entries, and case progress changes shall be synchronized across authorized user dashboards within 2 seconds of successful submission using real-time communication mechanisms. 
NFR-PERF-004: Concurrent User CapacityThe system shall support at least 144 concurrent active users without performance degradation exceeding 20% of baseline response times. 
NFR-PERF-005: Document Access PerformanceSupporting documents, including PDF and image-based attachments, shall begin loading within 3 seconds of authorized access requests through optimized cloud-based content delivery services. 
NFR-PERF-006: Report Generation PerformanceStandard dashboards, analytics summaries, and exportable reports for routine date ranges shall be generated within 10 seconds. 
NFR-PERF-007: System Availability During Operational HoursThe system shall maintain an availability target of at least 99% uptime, excluding scheduled maintenance windows. 
NFR-PERF-008: Scalability PerformanceThe system architecture shall support future expansion in the number of partner agencies, users, case volume, and reporting complexity without requiring major system redesign. 
Performance Assumptions

stable internet connectivity of 15–30 Mbps

5.2 Safety Requirements
The following safety requirements are intended to minimize operational risks, prevent accidental misuse, and protect the integrity of OFW case handling workflows in alignment with the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 quality model.
NFR-SAFE-001: Confirmation for Critical ActionsThe system shall require explicit user confirmation before executing critical or irreversible actions, including case closure, referral issuance, record deactivation, and other high-impact administrative actions. 
NFR-SAFE-002: Historical Record IntegrityThe system shall preserve case histories, referral logs, milestone entries, and audit records using an append-only mechanism that prevents unauthorized modification or deletion of historical records.

5.5 Business Rules
The following business rules define the operating principles and governance constraints for the Bayanihan One Window system. These rules ensure that system interactions follow the institutional mandates of DMW Region VII and the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 standards for safety and security.
BR-001: Mandatory Centralized Intake Official case intake for distressed OFWs shall be initiated solely by the Department of Migrant Workers (DMW) Region VII. This operationalizes the "Single-Entry" mechanism where a DMW Case Manager is the only role authorized to generate the initial Unified Master Case File.
BR-002: Single Source of Truth Each distressed OFW case must be maintained under a single Unified Master Case File serving as the official shared reference for all participating agencies. This rule implies a functional requirement for the system to automatically generate unique Case and Tracker Numbers to prevent record fragmentation.
BR-003: Parallel Referral AuthorityThe DMW Case Manager may initiate multiple digital referrals to various partner agencies simultaneously. This allows the system to support a multi-disciplinary service plan (e.g., legal, medical, and livelihood) without requiring sequential processing.
BR-004: Authorized Referral Routing Digital referrals shall be routed only to the system’s established partner network, which includes OWWA, DOLE, TESDA, DSWD, DOH, Law Center Inc., and specific LGUs in Bohol, Cebu, and Siquijor.
BR-005: Lane-Based Data Isolation Partner agencies shall have access only to the referrals and data assigned to their designated administrative "lane" or column. This is enforced via Row-Level Security (RLS) to ensure that one agency cannot view or edit the internal interventions of another.
BR-006: Mandatory Explanatory Remarks All high-stakes referral actions—including acceptance, rejection, or returning a case—shall require a mandatory text remark before the action can be submitted. This ensures transparency and provides a clear narrative for DMW oversight.
BR-007: Append-Only Operational Policy The system shall follow an immutable, append-only policy for all milestone entries, status updates, and audit logs. Any corrections to a recorded entry must be submitted as a new progress record rather than an edit to a historical record to maintain a non-repudiable history.
BR-008: Exclusive Closure Authority Only authorized DMW personnel have the system privilege to move a case to the final "Closed" status. A case is ineligible for closure until the system validates that all linked referrals have reached a terminal state (Completed, Rejected, or Unable to Proceed).
BR-009: Multi-Factor Authentication (OTP) Requirement Every system access attempt—whether by administrative staff or an OFW tracking their case—shall require a One-Time Password (OTP) sent to the user's registered email.
BR-010: Administrative Backend Restriction Access to the administrative dashboards (DMW, Agency, and Administrator views) is restricted to authorized office networks via IP Address Whitelisting. This prevents unauthorized access from external or foreign networks, given the sensitive nature of migrant worker data.
BR-011: Privacy-Safe Public Tracking OFWs are permitted to view real-time status updates through a public tracking portal using their Tracker Number and OTP. However, this interface shall not expose confidential internal agency comments, sensitive notes, or restricted attachments.
BR-012: Duplicate Record Flagging During the intake process, the system must perform a duplicate check based on the client's name and birthdate; any potential matches must be flagged for administrative review before a new case can be finalized.
BR-013: Data Governance for Analytics Closed case data and operational records may be used for analytics and reporting to support data-driven policy planning, provided all processing complies with the Data Privacy Act of 2012.

Appendix A: Glossary
Acronyms and Abbreviations
ACID: Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, and Durability; a set of properties that guarantee reliable database transactions.
AGY / AGCY: Agency; refers to the partner government or non-government organisations involved in the referral network.
API: Application Programming Interface; a set of protocols for building and integrating application software.
CDN: Content Delivery Network; a system of distributed servers that deliver web content, such as documents and images, with high performance and availability (e.g., Cloudinary).
DMW: Department of Migrant Workers; the primary government agency responsible for protecting the rights and welfare of OFWs.
DOH: Department of Health.
DOLE: Department of Labor and Employment.
DSWD: Department of Social Welfare and Development.
HTTPS: Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure; an extension of HTTP for secure communication over a computer network.
ISO/IEC 25010:2023: The international standard for systems and software quality requirements and evaluation.
JSONB: JavaScript Object Notation Binary; a structured data type used for storing audit logs and previous/new data values in the database.
LGU: Local Government Unit; includes provincial and city-level government offices in Region VII.
MVC: Model-View-Controller; a software architectural pattern used to separate internal logic from the user interface.
OFW: Overseas Filipino Worker; a Filipino citizen who works abroad under legal employment arrangements.
OTP: One-Time Password; a unique, time-sensitive security code used for multi-factor authentication.
OWWA: Overseas Workers Welfare Administration.
RBAC: Role-Based Access Control; a security mechanism that restricts system access to authorized users based on their specific roles.
RLS: Row-Level Security; a database security feature used to enforce "lane-based" isolation by limiting data visibility based on user policies.
SRS: Software Requirements Specification; this document.
SSL/TLS: Secure Sockets Layer / Transport Layer Security; cryptographic protocols designed to provide communications security over a computer network.
TESDA: Technical Education and Skills Development Authority.
UUID: Universally Unique Identifier; a 128-bit number used to uniquely identify information in the database (e.g., USER_ID, CASE_ID).
WAF: Web Application Firewall; a security layer that filters and monitors HTTP traffic to protect against application-layer attacks.
Key System Terms
Append-Only Audit Trail: A non-repudiable history of system activities where records are added but never edited or deleted, ensuring full traceability and accountability.
Bayanihan One Window System: A web-based platform designed to centralize case management, referral coordination, and tracking for repatriated and distressed OFWs.
Case Intake: The initial "single-entry" process performed by DMW to collect and record information about an OFW seeking assistance.
Case Management: The systematic process of organizing, monitoring, and coordinating services provided to OFWs from intake to final resolution.
Lane-Based Tracking: A structural design where each participating agency manages and views only the specific referral and milestone data assigned to their administrative "lane" or column.
Milestone: An append-only progress entry recorded by an agency to document specific actions, updates, or notes related to a referral.
Repatriation: The process of returning OFWs to the Philippines due to emergencies, employment termination, labor disputes, or other distress circumstances.
Single Source of Truth: A centralized data persistence model where a single unified record serves as the authoritative reference for all agencies, preventing data isolation.
Terminal Status: A final referral state indicating that an intervention is no longer active; limited to "Completed," "Rejected," or "Unable to Proceed".
Tracker Number: A unique, system-generated identifier provided to the OFW to monitor their case status through the public portal without requiring a full system account.
Unified Master Case File: A centralized record established during intake that captures a client's profile, narrative, and service needs as a shared reference for all referred agencies.
